Project Proposal

Developing Personas

Rationale

To develop our personas we followed the method outlined in Chapter 4 of Mental Models, "Define Task-Based Audience Segments".

  1. We started by listing distinguishing tasks, due to the relatively short amount of time we had to do observations, we also added in some characteristic behaviours that we have seen in friends and family with respect to social media and electronic device usage.
  2. The next step was to group the task, this step is really where our personas started taking shape as we saw the more addictive traits fall under our "Avid User" persona and the traits that involved using social media as a coping mechanism fall under the "Reluctant User".
  3. The third step was to name the groups or personas and we found that quite simple because our observations had quite a distinctive split between the "Avid user" and the "Reluctant user".

Personas

Luna Lovegood: The Avid User

Luna represents the avid phone user that we observed and have all seen in day to day life. She is never without an electronic device, either a phone, laptop or ipad and is constantly plugged into social media. She has notifications enabled for all her websites and appllications so as to never miss anything. She is always talking about how she should go on a technology detox but is consumed by FOMO. On average she spends 12-15 hours on her electronic devices. She is always comparing her life with those of the influencers she follows on social media.


Neville Longbottom: The Reluctant User

Neville has some mental health problems so has made efforts to cut back on social media usage. He struggles with stopping because he doesn't really think he has a social media addiction so sees no need to change. Social media is a tool for him to experience "normal life" because he struggles with leaving the house and interacting with people when he's having bad days. The internet and particularly social media provide escape for Neville. He believes that he's better off living vicariously through social media because he finds it easier to socialize when not face to face.

Finding Related Products


Related Product #1: MoodKit is a smartphone app designed for use with iOS 7.1 or later. It is based on the principal of using cognitive behavioural therapy to improve the mood, anxiety, and stress levels of the user. The app contains four sections labelled, “Activities,” “Thoughts,” “Mood,” and “Journal.” Within these sections, the user can enter specific goals or thoughts, similar to the function of a diary. It is also possible for the user to select activities from a provided list that are suggested to improve moods. MoodKit’s other feature is that it will offer tips to the user on how they can improve their mood.



Different functionalities offered by MoodKit

Benefits:

  • App is based on CBT, which is proven to be an effective method for improving moods.


    • Drawbacks:

  • Not the most interesting application to use as it is very text based, rather than game-like.
  • Due to all the different areas to enter information on the app, it requires significant personal motivation to use it. This is unlikely to contribute to the longevity of the app.
  • It costs $4.99 on the Apple app store which may discourage users.

  • MoodKit is not available to Android users.



    • Related Product #2: QuantiModo is a chrome browser extension that collects, aggregates, and analyzes data on symptoms, diet, sleep, exercise, medication etc. It then gives advice on how to maximize your health and happiness. It is also available as an app for iPhone and Android users.



    Available platforms offered by QuantiModo

    Benefits:

  • Provides a central place to track your moods and medications rather than having all your information spread out across multiple applications.
  • Is a browser extension, therefore the user doesn’t have to open an app every time they want to access the program.




    • Drawbacks:

  • Plugin is not very streamlined in execution. The reviews for the program show that there are still a lot of bugs. For example, the app allows you to schedule notifications, but the notifications do not consistently arrive for the user, which defies the point of the extension.




    • Comparing Products


    Related Product #1: There are similarities between iMoody and MoodKit, in that they are both based around the idea of cognitive behavioural therapy, however there are several reasons as to why iMoody is the superior option for university students:

  • iMoody is designed to be simple and fast to use. While MoodKit offers a more journal like experience, iMoody offers a quick questionnaire to determine your mood and feelings, therefore it is a faster experience for the user and more suited to a busy student.
  • iMoody is a chrome extension, so it is more convenient for students who are often at their computers. An app may seem easy, but the user would have to log on at several different time periods to track their mood on MoodKit, so iMoody offers a simple alternative.
  • iMoody is a better option for lower income users as it is free to use, versus MoodKit which is $4.99 to download.




    • Related Product #2: iMoody is a superior alternative to QuantiModo for use by university students.

  • It uses a different psychological approach. QuantiModo uses mindfulness, which is a therapeutic technique attained by purposely focusing on awareness of the present moment. iMoody uses cognitive behavioural therapy, which, unlike mindfulness, is a short term option for dealing with anxiety and mental health issues. The basic concept of CBT is that focusing on changing the way you think will change the way you behave. As our product is aiming at younger people addicted to technology, we feel like CBT is a better approach more suited to their needs.
  • iMoody is a simple concept, so there will be less issues in the functionality of the extension unlike QuantiModo which has problems with bugs in the development.
  • iMoody is better suited to already overwhelmed students, as it is only available as a chrome extension, unlike QuanitModo, which is available in several different forms. This will make it more simple for the user to add once to their computer, rather than having to download it across multiple platforms.
